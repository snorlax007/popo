# Popo — Marketing Pack
*AiNa Stage 7: Brand strategy, copy, and launch plan*

---

## Brand Positioning

**Brand Archetype:** The Companion — warm, playful, a little mysterious.

**One-line pitch:**
> *Popo: the AI desk companion that speaks its own language.*

**Tagline options:**
1. "Give your desk a soul."
2. "Not a speaker. A companion."
3. "It doesn't speak English. It speaks Popo."

**Tone of voice:** Warm, whimsical, confident. Never corporate. Never "AI-powered".
Think Pixar × Teenage Engineering × early Apple. The product is a character, not a device.

## Key Messages by Phase

### Phase 0 (Pre-launch / validation)
- "We're making something weird. And we need your help."
- Behind-the-scenes: recording the Popish language, breadboard prototype videos.
- Goal: 500+ early access signups, 80%+ charm-rate on voice tests.

### Phase 1–2 (Alpha / prototype)
- Teaser video: Popo reacting to "Hey Popo, I'm stressed" → sad face + blue LEDs + "Poo-po…"
- Focus on the *language* — it's the hook no competitor has.
- Press: "We built an AI that refuses to speak English" (story angle for Wired, The Verge).

### Phase 3–4 (Beta / mobile app)
- "Meet Popo" launch video (60 seconds, no voiceover, just Popish).
- Beta user testimonials: mood history screenshots, "my Popo knows me better than Siri."
- Crowdfunding page (Kickstarter / Indiegogo) if opted for pre-order model.

### Phase 5 (Production)
- Product Hunt launch (aim for Product of the Day).
- Gift guide placement ($99 price point = premium gift territory).
- Holiday campaign: "The gift that feels alive."

## Pricing Strategy (Recommendation)

**$99 one-time** for hardware (answers PRD §16 Q3).

Rationale:
- $79 + $5/mo risks churn perception for a physical product.
- $99 one-time is gift-friendly, impulse-buyable, and positions Popo as premium not subscription-tired.
- Cloud AI costs at 50-device Phase 4 scale are ~$15/year total (Claude Haiku). No margin pressure.
- Reserve subscription for a future "Popo Pro" tier (personality packs, smart home integrations, cloud history sync).

## Competitive Differentiation

| Competitor | What they do | Popo's edge |
|---|---|---|
| Amazon Echo | Assistant, smart home | Popo isn't a tool. It's a companion with a personality. |
| Tamagotchi | Toy, nostalgia | Popo is AI-native, reacts to real speech, lives on your desk. |
| Lovot (GROOVE X) | Robot companion | Popo is $99, not $3,000. |
| Nothing Ear / Teenage Engineering | Aesthetic gadgets | Popo has behavior, not just aesthetics. |

**The real moat:** No one else has the Popish language. A competitor can copy the hardware.
They can't copy a voice actor recording session, a phoneme sequencer, and 2 years of character development.
